## Trial Period Simulation

### What You'll See

An interactive preview of how trial fostering works to test compatibility before adoption.

**How It Works (3 Steps)**
```
1. Short-term Fostering
   Take a pet home for 2-7 days

2. Daily Check-ins
   Complete guided feedback forms

3. Informed Decision
   Adopt or return without pressure
```

**Your Choices:**
- Interested in trial period? Yes / No
- Preferred duration:
  - Weekend (2-3 days)
  - One week (7 days)
  - Two weeks (14 days)

**Interactive Simulation:**
Click "Start Trial Simulation" to experience a Day 2 check-in form:
- How is the pet adjusting?
- Any concerns? (allergies, behavior, energy, space, time)
- Additional notes about your experience

**Why It Matters:**
- Reduces return rates by revealing issues early
- Tests for real-world allergies and behavioral fit
- Gives confidence before final commitment
- Helps shelter improve future matches

## Example Costs by Pet Type

### Dogs
- **Small Dog** (under 20 lbs)
  - Monthly: $125
  - Annual: $1,500
  - Emergency: $2,000

- **Medium Dog** (20-50 lbs)
  - Monthly: $175
  - Annual: $2,100
  - Emergency: $3,000

- **Large Dog** (over 50 lbs)
  - Monthly: $225
  - Annual: $2,700
  - Emergency: $4,000

### Cats
- **Small/Medium Cat**
  - Monthly: $85-95
  - Annual: $1,020-1,140
  - Emergency: $1,500-1,800

*Note: Costs include food, supplies, routine vet care. Special needs pets may cost more.*

## Key Benefits

### For Adopters
- Realistic financial expectations before commitment
- Trial period reduces anxiety about the decision
- Better understanding of long-term responsibilities
- Confidence in their ability to provide proper care

### For Shelters
- Reduced return rates (20-30% returns are financially motivated)
- Better quality matches through informed adopters
- Feedback from trials improves future matching
- Higher success rate for long-term placements

### For Pets
- Permanent homes with prepared families
- Less trauma from returns
- Better quality of life with financially stable owners
- Reduced shelter overcrowding
